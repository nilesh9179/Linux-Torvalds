Biography

Linus Torvalds is a Finnish-American software engineer best known for creating the Linux kernel, the foundational software for the Linux operating system. He also created the version control system Git. Born on December 28, 1969, in Helsinki, Finland, Torvalds developed Linux as a student project in 1991, aiming to create a free, open-source operating system.

Achievements

  • 1991: Started developing the Linux kerne
  • 1996: Asteroid 9793 Torvalds was named in his honor.
  • 1997: Received honorary doctorates from Stockholm University and the University of Helsinki.
  • 1998: Received the EFF Pioneer Award.
  • 1999: Voted one of the "most important people" of the 20th century by Time magazine.
  • 2000: Awarded the Lovelace Medal by the British Computer Society.
  • 2001: Shared the Takeda Award for Social/Economic Well-Being.
  • 2005: Created Git
  • 2008: Inducted into the Hall of Fellows of the Computer History Museum.
  • 2010: Awarded the C&C Prize by the NEC Corporation.
  • 2012: Shared the Millennium Technology Prize.
  • 2014: Received the IEEE Computer Society Computer Pioneer Award.
  • Received the ACM SIGOPS Hall of Fame Award.
  • 2021: Awarded the Takeda Foundation's 2021 Takeda Award for Social Contribution.
  • 2021: Received the 2021 IEEE Masaru Ibuka Consumer Electronics Award.
